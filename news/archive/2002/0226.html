Scamming the local radio
Wall Street Journal | Submitted by: Soylent
Now that large corporations are permitted to own many more radio stations than ever, they are running stations that pretend to be local and hope to fool the listening audience. For example, a Boise station owned by Clear Channel: "After the station went KISS on March 13, 2000, it began importing all of its DJs. Weekday mornings came from Los Angeles, middays from Cincinnati, afternoons from San Diego and evenings from Tampa, Fla. Two of the old rock station’s DJs were laid off. Later, one out-of-town KISS DJ moved to Boise to do a live afternoon show. As costs went down, ratings went up."
