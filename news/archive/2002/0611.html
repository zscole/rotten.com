The going-out-of-business scam
Straight Goods | Submitted by: Ann Onymous
It works like this. Say a furniture store needs a quick cash injection. The store hires a liquidation company masquerading as a business or a perhaps a marketing solutions company to handle all aspects of the sale. The store pays the liquidation company by jacking up its prices. The store will often close for a day or two to give the liquidators time to completely rewrite the prices, cross them out and advertise the new sale prices, which are in fact sometimes considerably higher than the regular prices. The store hangs on to the same margins as before, pays the liquidation company and makes a packet in a short period of time through sheer volume of customers. The sale lasts two or three months, the store then closes for a day or two, if at all and advertises a reopening sale a short time later.
