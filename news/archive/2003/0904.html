Building a better bird strike simulant
San Antonio Express-News | Submitted by: Reid Fleming
"The recipe for synthetic chicken starts with a vat of industrial gelatin, not too unlike the lime, cherry and lemon varieties sold in the grocery store. To that, the "chefs" at Southwest Research Institute add microspheres -- powder-sized capsules of air encased in plastic. The mixture then is poured into coffee can-sized molds and refrigerated, because synthetic chicken apparently will spoil and reek just like real meat if left out at room temperature. When the concoction has solidified, engineers at the institute have Federal Aviation Administration-approved fake birds ranging from 2 to 10 pounds -- a weight and mass that will mimic the impact of real birds when they are fired from the institute's giant compressed gas gun. The faux birds don't smell or taste much like chicken, but they splatter like the real thing when they are blown down the gun's 33-foot-long barrel and into revved-up airplane engines or propellers."
