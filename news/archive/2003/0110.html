No Habla Espanol
Santa Cruz Sentinel | Submitted by: Incubus
"The study, which comes out today in the journal Pediatrics, found an average of 31 errors per visit for Spanish-speaking patients using interpreters at pediatric clinics. Thirteen families were monitored. Errors were most common when ad hoc interpreters such as family members or untrained bilingual nurses were involved. Almost two-thirds of the errors had clinical consequences. Those included wrong instructions on dose and duration of prescribed drugs and omission of important information about patient drug allergies or medical history.
