EPA forced to lie to New Yorkers about 9-11 air quality
MSNBC | Submitted by: Crazy Quagga
"At the White House's direction, the Environmental Protection Agency gave New Yorkers misleading assurances that there was no health risk from the debris-laden air after the World Trade Center collapse, according to an internal inquiry. President Bush's senior environmental adviser on Friday defended the White House involvement, saying it was justified by national security. The White House 'convinced EPA to add reassuring statements and delete cautionary ones' ... 'When EPA made a Sept. 18 announcement that the air was "safe" to breathe, the agency did not have sufficient data and analyses to make the statement,' the report says, adding that the EPA had yet to adequately monitor air quality for contaminants such as PCBs, soot and dioxin. ... it wasn't until June 2002 that the EPA determined that air quality had returned to pre-Sept. 11 levels -- well after respiratory ailments and other problems began to surface in hundreds of workers cleaning dusty offices and apartments."

Pentagon needs HR director
KNSD-TV 7 San Diego | Submitted by: Tim Howard
"A congressional report says the Pentagon computer systems are having trouble keeping track of all members of the military. And, the report says, that flaw has hampered the activation of about 300,000 reservists since the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks. The report by the General Accounting Office says the military services have separate computer systems and not all of them are compatible. That meant some officials had to use desktop-computer spreadsheets to call up reservists. The investigators found that having inconsistent orders on how to activate units caused problems. For example, the Pentagon lost track of some reservists who had been called to active duty, kept some on active duty longer than authorized and kept units on alert to be activated for a year or more."

The Japanese Government has sent its condolences to the bereaved family and promised to continue its efforts to dispose of the dangerous chemicals. ... up to 2,000 people have died since the war after coming into contact with the weapons."
